OFFICIAL RULES

How to Play Discball

Master the rules. Dominate the field. Become a warrior.

Quick Overview

Discball is played with frisbees instead of dodgeballs. Two teams face off, attempting to eliminate opponents by hitting them with flying discs while dodging incoming throws. Last team standing wins!

Game Setup

Teams

3-8 players per team. More warriors = more chaos = more fun!

Equipment

4-6 frisbees (soft or standard). A rectangular playing field with a center line. Optional: boundaries marked with cones.

Playing Field

Similar to dodgeball - two halves separated by a center line. Each team starts on their side. Recommended: 30-50 feet per half.

How to Play

1

Game Start

Frisbees are placed on the center line. On "GO!", players rush to grab discs and return to their side.

2

Throwing

Throw frisbees at opponents. Any throw style is allowed - forehand, backhand, hammer, whatever works! You must stay on your side of the center line.

3

Getting Hit = OUT

If a frisbee hits you anywhere on your body (before touching the ground), you're OUT. Head to the sidelines immediately.

4

The Catch

CATCH a frisbee mid-air before it hits the ground? The thrower is OUT, and you can bring one eliminated teammate back in!

5

Winning

Eliminate all players on the opposing team to win the round. Best 2 out of 3 rounds wins the match!

Special Rules

🚫
No Headshots:Intentional headshots result in the thrower being OUT
⚡
Deflections:If a disc bounces off one player and hits another before touching the ground, the second player is also OUT
🎯
Stalling:No holding onto a frisbee for more than 10 seconds. Keep the action flowing!
⚔️
Warrior's Honor:Call your own outs. Honesty is part of the game

Pro Tips

  • →Communication is key: Call out incoming discs to your teammates
  • →Mix your throws: Vary speed and angles to keep opponents guessing
  • →Watch the eyes: Opponents often look where they're about to throw
  • →Protect your catchers: A good catch can turn the tide of the game
  • →Stay mobile: Standing still makes you an easy target

Safety First, Warriors Second

While bruises are badges of honor, serious injuries are not. Play hard but play smart:

  • • Use appropriate frisbees (not hard plastic tournament discs)
  • • Clear the playing area of hazards
  • • Warm up before playing
  • • Respect when someone calls a timeout for injury
  • • Have a first aid kit on hand
